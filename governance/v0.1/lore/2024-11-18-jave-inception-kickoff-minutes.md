## Agenda

1. Ratify initiative charter and executive sponsorship model.
2. Define why Orcal requires a new language platform.
3. Establish naming and governance posture for public rollout.
4. Assign accountable leadership for compiler, runtime, docs, and ecosystem operations.

## Executive Positioning

- CEO framed Jave as strategic unification across tooling, onboarding, and enterprise platform control.
- CTO established architecture principles: deterministic behavior, modular compiler phases, portable runtime.
- COO required milestone governance and explicit freeze gates.
- CSO mandated security language, trustworthy diagnostics, and incident-usable output pathways.
- VP Engineering accepted implementation accountability and feasibility governance.
- VP People required process rituals and documentation posture that could scale across teams.

## Core Decisions

- Initiative approved as an executive-sponsored Orcal program.
- Product name convention approved: "Orcal Jave" externally, "Jave" in technical usage.
- Governance body approved: Jave Steering Committee with rotating technical and product observers.
- Naming strategy approved: intentional ceremonial language with stable semantic behavior.
- Toolchain triad approved: `javec`, `baggage`, `javevm`.

## Feature Direction (Initial)

- Entrypoint model to include ceremonial bootstrap and runtime entry naming.
- Structured control flow with worded operators and committee-scarred syntax.
- Standard library pathing to follow educationally framed namespace strategy.
- Runtime must prioritize deterministic output over performance aesthetics.
- Compatibility posture must tolerate selected legacy aliases where ecosystem disruption risk is high.

## Action Items

- A1: Draft v0.1 language spec boundaries and freeze criteria.
- A2: Produce initial parser/runtime proof set and examples.
- A3: Draft governance artifacts for every major design decision.
- A4: Prepare launch posture that balances corporate stewardship and community readability.

## Closing Statement

The executive council confirmed Jave as a chartered platform initiative with direct C-Suite sponsorship, delegated implementation leadership, and standing governance obligations.
